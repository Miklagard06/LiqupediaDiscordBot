<p><b>OG</b> was created as the new team of former <a href="/dota2/(monkey)_Business" title="(monkey) Business">(monkey) Business</a> players. The team's breakthrough occurred at the  <a href="/dota2/Frankfurt_Major/2015" title="Frankfurt Major/2015">Frankfurt Major</a> in late November, 2015. They fought through the lower bracket, defeating <a class="mw-redirect" href="/dota2/TI5" title="TI5">TI5</a> champions and runner-ups, <a href="/dota2/Evil_Geniuses" title="Evil Geniuses">Evil Geniuses</a> and <a href="/dota2/CDEC_Gaming" title="CDEC Gaming">CDEC Gaming</a>. By winning the <a href="/dota2/Kiev_Major/2017" title="Kiev Major/2017">2017 Kiev Major</a>, OG became the first team in Dota 2 to win four <a href="/dota2/Dota_Major_Championships" title="Dota Major Championships">Dota Major Championships</a>. In August 2018, OG became the first team to win <a class="mw-redirect" href="/dota2/The_International_2018" title="The International 2018">The International</a> from an open qualifier. The final of the event was the subject of a 2018 episode of "True Sight", making OG the only team to be featured in the series more than once. In August 2019, OG became the first team to win more than one TI by winning <a class="mw-redirect" href="/dota2/TI9" title="TI9">TI9</a>, also becoming the first back-to-back TI winners in the history of the game.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/dota2/index.php?title=OG&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span id="2015/2016_Season:_From_Underdog_to_TI_Favorite"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2015.2F2016_Season:_From_Underdog_to_TI_Favorite">2015/2016 Season: From Underdog to TI Favorite</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/dota2/index.php?title=OG&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2015/2016 Season: From Underdog to TI Favorite">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Team OG was originally formed under the name <a href="/dota2/(monkey)_Business" title="(monkey) Business">(monkey) Business</a> and was comprised of both experienced veterans in <a href="/dota2/N0tail" title="N0tail">n0tail</a> and <a href="/dota2/Fly" title="Fly">Fly</a> as well as new and improving talents in <a href="/dota2/Miracle-" title="Miracle-">Miracle-</a>, <a href="/dota2/Cr1t-" title="Cr1t-">Cr1t-</a> and <a href="/dota2/MoonMeander" title="MoonMeander">MoonMeander</a>. The team achieved their breakthrough during the <a href="/dota2/Frankfurt_Major/2015" title="Frankfurt Major/2015">Frankfurt Major</a> defeating European powerhouse <a href="/dota2/Team_Secret" title="Team Secret">Team Secret</a> in the grand finals. The team continued to have a strong presence in the European Dota scene, however, after a fourth-place finish at <a href="/dota2/The_Summit/4" title="The Summit/4">The Summit 4</a>, OG's dominance began to wane and ended with a 7-8th place in the <a href="/dota2/Shanghai_Major/2016" title="Shanghai Major/2016">Shanghai Major</a>. Despite this, the team rebounded with a 3rd place finish at <a class="mw-redirect" href="/dota2/EPICENTER/Season_1" title="EPICENTER/Season 1">EPICENTER</a> and claimed a second Dota Major Championship title at the <a href="/dota2/Manila_Major/2016" title="Manila Major/2016">Manila Major</a>. The squad proceeded to defeat Natus Vincere at <a href="/dota2/ESL_One/Frankfurt/2016" title="ESL One/Frankfurt/2016">ESL One Frankfurt 2016</a>, winning the tournament and securing their invite to <a href="/dota2/The_International/2016" title="The International/2016">The International 2016</a>.
</p><p>OG performed well in the group stage of <a href="/dota2/The_International/2016" title="The International/2016">The International 2016</a> and finished first in their group. Despite this, the team was unable to maintain their momentum and fell to the loser's bracket against <a href="/dota2/MVP_Phoenix" title="MVP Phoenix">MVP Phoenix</a>. 7ckingmad would later say in his 'Reflections' interview with Duncan <a class="extiw" href="https://liquipedia.net/counterstrike/Thorin" title="counterstrike:Thorin">Thorin</a> Shields that they chose to play MVP Phoenix after their 'complete domination' of them at the Manila Major, and that this may have been a mistake. Following this unexpected loss the team was eliminated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/dota2/TnC_Gaming" title="TnC Gaming">TnC Gaming</a> and finished 9-12th, in what has been referred to as one of the biggest upsets in professional Dota 2,.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TNC_upset_OG_1-0"><a href="#cite_note-TNC_upset_OG-1">[1]</a></sup> Shortly after their disappointing run, OG announced that <a href="/dota2/MoonMeander" title="MoonMeander">MoonMeander</a> would no longer be an active player, and that <a href="/dota2/Cr1t-" title="Cr1t-">Cr1t-</a> and <a href="/dota2/Miracle-" title="Miracle-">Miracle-</a> would be leaving the team.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Change_is_never_easy_2-0"><a href="#cite_note-Change_is_never_easy-2">[2]</a></sup>. In that same 'Reflections' interview, 7ckingmad would claim that it was a personality conflict within the team that led to the removal of Moonmeander and that once the team wasn't remaining as 5 it prompted Miracle to consider other offers.
</p>
<h3><span id="2016/2017:_Reigning_Champions"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2016.2F2017:_Reigning_Champions">2016/2017: Reigning Champions</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/dota2/index.php?title=OG&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: 2016/2017: Reigning Champions">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>To replace <a href="/dota2/Cr1t-" title="Cr1t-">Cr1t-</a>, <a href="/dota2/Miracle-" title="Miracle-">Miracle-</a> and <a href="/dota2/MoonMeander" title="MoonMeander">MoonMeander</a>, OG added <a class="mw-redirect" href="/dota2/Jerax" title="Jerax">Jerax</a>, formerly of <a href="/dota2/Team_Liquid" title="Team Liquid">Team Liquid</a>, <a href="/dota2/Ana" title="Ana">Ana</a> and Alliance's <a href="/dota2/S4" title="S4">s4</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-New_Waves_3-0"><a href="#cite_note-New_Waves-3">[3]</a></sup> <a href="/dota2/S4" title="S4">s4</a> would take on the offlane role, which was a new position for him but with familiar heroes in his signature Puck, Batrider and Magnus. <a href="/dota2/Ana" title="Ana">Ana</a> came to the team from a substitute position for <a href="/dota2/Invictus_Gaming" title="Invictus Gaming">Invictus Gaming</a>. With the addition of these new players, OG made a strong start to the season by claiming first place at the Autumn Major in 2016, held in Boston USA, to become the first team to ever defend a Major title in Dota 2 history. The team was directly invited to the Winter Major for 2017, held in Kiev. After securing a top seed finish in the group stages (finishing 3-1), they battled their way to the Grand Finals beating <a href="/dota2/Team_Random" title="Team Random">Team Random</a>, <a href="/dota2/Evil_Geniuses" title="Evil Geniuses">Evil Geniuses</a> and ultimately <a class="mw-redirect" href="/dota2/Virtus_Pro" title="Virtus Pro">Virtus Pro</a> in the Grand Finals to claim the Mystic Staff. The Grand Finals series for the Kiev Major was the first Valve event Grand Finals to go to a game five since The International 3; with <a href="/dota2/S4" title="S4">s4</a> having played in both. 
</p><p>Afterwards, OG seemed unable to maintain their rhythm, finishing <a href="/dota2/The_Masters/2017/Manila" title="The Masters/2017/Manila">The Manila Masters</a> in 5th-6th place and failing to qualify for the main event of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/dota2/EPICENTER/Season_2" title="EPICENTER/Season 2">EPICENTER 2017</a>. Nevertheless, they once again entered the year's <a href="/dota2/The_International/2017" title="The International/2017">International</a> as strong favourites for the title. However, in keeping with their slowdown from the few months prior, OG failed to make it to the upper bracket in the group stages and was ultimately eliminated by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/dota2/LGD" title="LGD">LGD</a>.
</p><p>OG dominated the competitive Dota 2 scene during the era of Valve hosted Major Championships between TI5 and TI7. During this period OG won four of the seven official <a href="/dota2/Dota_Major_Championships" title="Dota Major Championships">Dota Major Championships</a>, cementing their status as legends of the game. OG currently holds several world records in Dota 2 as the only team to win multiple Dota Major Championship titles as well as claiming consecutive Major victories and defending a Major title as returning champions. Despite this, the team would have to wait another year and undergo more roster changes before they could claim the Aegis of Champions.
</p>
<h3><span id="2017/2018:_Dota_Pro_Circuit"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2017.2F2018:_Dota_Pro_Circuit">2017/2018: Dota Pro Circuit</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/dota2/index.php?title=OG&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: 2017/2018: Dota Pro Circuit">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>With Valve's new <a href="/dota2/Dota_Pro_Circuit" title="Dota Pro Circuit">Dota Pro Circuit</a> system, OG looked to once again set themselves up as a strong contender for <a class="mw-redirect" href="/dota2/The_International_2018" title="The International 2018">The International 2018</a>. After <a href="/dota2/Ana" title="Ana">ana</a> left the team to take a break from competitive Dota<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ana_break_4-0"><a href="#cite_note-ana_break-4">[4]</a></sup>, <span class="flag"><a href="/dota2/Category:Ukraine" title="Ukraine"><img alt="Ukraine" height="24" loading="lazy" src="/commons/images/b/bb/Ua_hd.png" width="36"/></a></span> <a href="/dota2/Ukraine" title="Ukraine">Ukranian</a> player <a href="/dota2/Resolut1on" title="Resolut1on">Resolut1on</a> joined the squad to fill the vacancy. With their new addition, OG dove into the qualifiers for the first events of the Pro Circuit, however the team were unable to qualify for the first three events. They only came close to qualifying in the last qualifier, for <a href="/dota2/StarLadder/i-League_Invitational/3/Europe" title="StarLadder/i-League Invitational/3/Europe">StarLadder Season 3</a>, where they lost in the grand finals to <a href="/dota2/Team_Secret" title="Team Secret">Team Secret</a>. 
</p><p>Their first qualifier victory and confirmed LAN attendance would be for <a href="/dota2/Dota_Pit_League/Season_6" title="Dota Pit League/Season 6">Dota PIT</a> by the end of September 2017. Despite narrowly failing to make the top four at the LAN, and thus securing themselves qualifying points for TI8, OG's results would soon start to look better. By November, they had secured two more qualifiers, for <a href="/dota2/Mars_Dota_2_League/Macau/2017/Europe" title="Mars Dota 2 League/Macau/2017/Europe">MDL Macau</a> and <a href="/dota2/Captains_Draft/4/Europe" title="Captains Draft/4/Europe">Captains Draft 4.0</a>, and had received an invite to <a href="/dota2/The_Summit/8" title="The Summit/8">DOTA Summit 8</a>. All three events, taking part in December 2017 and early January 2018, would yield OG qualifying points, as the team reached the semifinals for Captains Draft and DOTA Summit and even winning MDL Macau, ahead of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/dota2/TNC_Pro_Team" title="TNC Pro Team">TNC</a>, for their first LAN victory of the new season. Throughout early 2018, the team's results would finally meet expectations with OG dominating the European qualifier scene, with successful qualifications for <a href="/dota2/ESL_One/Katowice/2018/Europe" title="ESL One/Katowice/2018/Europe">ESL One Katowice</a>, <a href="/dota2/PGL/Bucharest_Major/2018/Europe" title="PGL/Bucharest Major/2018/Europe">The Bucharest Major</a>, <a href="/dota2/DreamLeague/Season_9/Europe" title="DreamLeague/Season 9/Europe">DreamLeague Season 9</a>, <a href="/dota2/Dota_2_Asia_Championships/2018/Europe" title="Dota 2 Asia Championships/2018/Europe">DAC 2018</a>, and <a href="/dota2/EPICENTER/2018/Europe" title="EPICENTER/2018/Europe">EPICENTER XL</a>. ESL One Katowice would be team's first LAN appearance at a Major in the new Pro Circuit system.
</p><p>Sadly, results at their first two Major LAN appearances of 2018, at <a href="/dota2/ESL_One/Katowice/2018" title="ESL One/Katowice/2018">ESL One Katowice</a> and <a href="/dota2/PGL/Bucharest_Major/2018" title="PGL/Bucharest Major/2018">The Bucharest Major</a>, in February and March, respectively, were very disappointing. The team failed to break out of the group stage at both events. At ESL, using a double elimination group system, OG would narrowly miss qualifying for the main event, losing 0:2 to rivals <a href="/dota2/Team_Liquid" title="Team Liquid">Team Liquid</a> in the lower bracket final of their group. The Bucharest Major featured a Swiss group stage, where OG were once again knocked out by Team Liquid in the last round of the group stage. After returning from their LANs, the team would reflect upon their poor LAN results and in the following days coach <a href="/dota2/7ckngMad" title="7ckngMad">7ckngMad</a> would be seen playing for the team instead of Resolut1on. Days later, Resolut1on's departure from the team was confirmed<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Resolut1on_leaves_5-0"><a href="#cite_note-Resolut1on_leaves-5">[5]</a></sup> and OG would no longer be eligible to be directly invited to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/dota2/The_International_2018" title="The International 2018">The International 2018</a> nor for the regional qualifiers. They would have to qualify all the way through the open qualifiers. 
</p><p>Despite the roster change, the team easily won <a href="/dota2/Mars_Dota_2_League/Changsha_Major/2018/Europe" title="Mars Dota 2 League/Changsha Major/2018/Europe">MDL Changsha</a> qualifier. Mad would continue as their stand-in for the <a href="/dota2/DreamLeague/Season_9" title="DreamLeague/Season 9">DreamLeague</a> LAN event, where the team once again narrowly missed a top four spot, falling to Newbee in the lower bracket and finishing in 5-6th place. However, their result at <a href="/dota2/Dota_2_Asia_Championships/2018" title="Dota 2 Asia Championships/2018">DAC 2018</a> was very disappointing, as they were narrowly eliminated in the group stage after a tiebreaker loss to <a href="/dota2/Vici_Gaming" title="Vici Gaming">Vici Gaming</a>. With another qualifier win for <a href="/dota2/ESL_One/Birmingham/2018" title="ESL One/Birmingham/2018">ESL One Birmingham</a> and an invite to the <a href="/dota2/Supermajor/2018" title="Supermajor/2018">China Dota 2 Supermajor</a>, OG would be attending the last four Majors of the season. At <a href="/dota2/EPICENTER/2018" title="EPICENTER/2018">EPICENTER XL</a>, the team surprisingly managed to dominate their group and secure an upper bracket spot, but failed to win a single map in the playoffs and once again narrowly missed a top 4 spot. <a href="/dota2/Mars_Dota_2_League/Changsha_Major/2018" title="Mars Dota 2 League/Changsha Major/2018">MDL Changsha</a> saw the team once again have a good group stage, narrowly securing an upper bracket spot. However, they were yet again unable to win a single map in the playoffs and finished 7-8th after being swept by both <a href="/dota2/VGJ.Storm" title="VGJ.Storm">VGJ.Storm</a> and <a href="/dota2/Newbee" title="Newbee">Newbee</a>. 
</p><p>After another disappointingly early exit at <a href="/dota2/ESL_One/Birmingham/2018" title="ESL One/Birmingham/2018">ESL One Birmingham</a>, despite topping their group, losing out to <a href="/dota2/Fnatic" title="Fnatic">Fnatic</a> in the first round of the playoffs, the scene was shocked by the departure of both <a href="/dota2/S4" title="S4">s4</a> and <a href="/dota2/Fly" title="Fly">Fly</a> to <a href="/dota2/Evil_Geniuses" title="Evil Geniuses">Evil Geniuses</a>. The latter had been a founding member of the original <a class="mw-selflink selflink">OG</a>/<a href="/dota2/(monkey)_Business" title="(monkey) Business">(monkey) Business</a> team and had been present for every single one of the teams four Major victories. As a result, the remaining members decided to pull out of the <a href="/dota2/Supermajor/2018" title="Supermajor/2018">China Dota 2 Supermajor</a>, the last Major of the season, in order to find players to complete their roster. Due to <a href="/dota2/Resolut1on" title="Resolut1on">Resolut1on</a>'s departure the team already was not eligible for an invite to <a class="mw-redirect" href="/dota2/The_International_2018" title="The International 2018">The International 2018</a> or for the regional qualifiers. In early June 2018, with some time remaining before the open qualifier would start, the team was able to announce a full roster with <span class="flag"><a href="/dota2/Category:Finland" title="Finland"><img alt="Finland" height="24" loading="lazy" src="/commons/images/0/07/Fi_hd.png" width="36"/></a></span> Finnish pubstar <a href="/dota2/Topson" title="Topson">Topson</a> joining and <a href="/dota2/Ana" title="Ana">ana</a> returning to the team. As a result, 7ckngMad would play offlane and N0tail would move to the hard support position.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="The_International_2018:_A_Dota_2_Cinderella_Story">The International 2018: A Dota 2 Cinderella Story</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/dota2/index.php?title=OG&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: The International 2018: A Dota 2 Cinderella Story">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>TI8 was looming, but OG still had a tough road ahead: the brutal <abbr title="Best-of-one">Bo1</abbr> gauntlet of the Open Qualifiers followed by the Regional Qualifiers. Due to the instability of the European scene in the past season, the Open Qualifiers were unusually stacked, yet OG managed to easily secure a Regional Qualifier spot through the first Open Qualifier. The team showed even more promise during throughout the Regional Qualifiers, ending the Group Stage without dropping a single game and taking the direct route to the finals in the playoff bracket. There they would meet another new team in <a href="/dota2/Wind_and_Rain" title="Wind and Rain">Wind and Rain</a>, whom they defeated 3-1 on the backs of Topson's <a href="/dota2/Pugna" title="Pugna">Pugna</a> and ana's <a href="/dota2/Phantom_Lancer" title="Phantom Lancer">Phantom Lancer</a>, to become the only European Qualifier team at TI8. 
</p><p>Placed into Group A, alongside DPC giants <a href="/dota2/Team_Liquid" title="Team Liquid">Team Liquid</a>, <a href="/dota2/PSG.LGD" title="PSG.LGD">PSG.LGD</a>, and <a href="/dota2/Mineski" title="Mineski">Mineski</a>, OG's task at TI was looking daunting. It would not get any easier after disappointing results in the first day of the Group Stage: despite taking a game off of favorites PSG.LGD, they were later swept by <a href="/dota2/Team_Liquid" title="Team Liquid">Team Liquid</a>. As a result, after two days of the group stage, OG found themselves in the lower half of Group A. But a fantastic third day in the group, going 5-1 in total, dropping only a single game to <a href="/dota2/Fnatic" title="Fnatic">Fnatic</a>, would prove to be enough to just secure OG a top 4 finish in their group and thus a spot in the Upper Bracket of The International 2018 Main Event. 
</p><p>Group B winners <a href="/dota2/VGJ.Storm" title="VGJ.Storm">VGJ.Storm</a> naturally chose OG as their opponent in the first round of the Upper Bracket, as OG was still looking like an underdog. However, OG shocked their opponents, beating them in two straight games. Still, OG was considered the underdog for the next round against <a href="/dota2/Evil_Geniuses" title="Evil Geniuses">Evil Geniuses</a>, where they would meet up against former teammates <a href="/dota2/Fly" title="Fly">Fly</a> and <a href="/dota2/S4" title="S4">s4</a>. Not unwarranted, however, as EG had been showing a fantastic performance up until this point. But it would not be enough against OG, who beat them 2-1 in an intense series for a spot in the Upper Bracket Final against heavy favorites <a href="/dota2/PSG.LGD" title="PSG.LGD">PSG.LGD</a>. And again, against all odds, OG would prevail and secure their spot in the Grand Final in another intense, nail-biting series against PSG.LGD: Game 3 had OG with their backs against the wall and LGD looked prime to take their <a href="/dota2/Buildings#Ancients" title="Buildings">Ancient</a>. With ana's <a href="/dota2/Spectre" title="Spectre">Spectre</a> down without <a class="mw-redirect" href="/dota2/Buyback" title="Buyback">buyback</a>, it would be Topson's <a href="/dota2/Arc_Warden" title="Arc Warden">Arc Warden</a> that almost single-handidly defended against three LGD members and led the chase after the remaining two LGD members attempted to back off. With few heroes left, LGD was unable to stop OG's assault on their own Ancient. 
</p><p>The Grand Finals would see a rematch with <a href="/dota2/PSG.LGD" title="PSG.LGD">PSG.LGD</a>, that would only be the second time a <a href="/dota2/The_International" title="The International">TI</a> Grand Final would go to the full five games. OG took game one on the backs of Topson's signature <a href="/dota2/Monkey_King" title="Monkey King">Monkey King</a> and ana's Spectre. But LGD would hit back hard, taking back-to-back games and putting OG's backs against the wall once again in Game 4: after a relatively even early and mid-game, LGD took a lead after consecutive kills on ana's <a href="/dota2/Phantom_Lancer" title="Phantom Lancer">Phantom Lancer</a> and Topson's <a href="/dota2/Invoker" title="Invoker">Invoker</a> and were able to secure two lanes of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/dota2/Barracks" title="Barracks">Barracks</a>. But mistakes on LGD's part and OG's fantastic teamfight execution in LGD's safelane resulted in <a href="/dota2/Ame" title="Ame">Ame</a>'s <a href="/dota2/Morphling" title="Morphling">Morphling</a> being dead for two minutes without buyback and OG able to take their own lane of Barracks. From that moment onwards OG was firmly in the lead, but a clutch Blink-<a href="/dota2/Axe#Berserker.27s_Call" title="Axe">Call</a> by <a class="mw-redirect" href="/dota2/Ceb" title="Ceb">Ceb</a> would secure OG's comeback with three members of LGD dead without buybacks. LGD did not let themselves be shaken from that game, though, as they came out swinging in the final game of the Grand Finals: on the back of <a href="/dota2/Fy" title="Fy">fy</a>'s fantastic rotations on <a href="/dota2/Earthshaker" title="Earthshaker">Earthshaker</a>, LGD was able to take a sizable lead after the early game. But once again it would be OG's superior teamfight execution, this time around <a href="/dota2/Roshan" title="Roshan">Roshan</a>'s pit, allowed OG to take a commanding position and finally the game. With that OG were crowned as the champions of The International 2018. They took the long way from the Open Qualifier and were considered underdogs throughout the entire event until the final game, but ultimately they showed the necessary resilience to come out on top. It would be the first time that the West was able to take two TI championships in a row, as the familiar East-West cycle of TI champions was finally broken.
</p>

<h3><span id="2018/19:"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="2018.2F19:">2018/19:</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/dota2/index.php?title=OG&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: 2018/19:">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In November 2018, <a href="/dota2/Ana" title="Ana">ana</a> left the team after deciding to take a break from the professional scene, with <a href="/dota2/Pajkatt" title="Pajkatt">Pajkatt</a> and <a href="/dota2/ILTW" title="ILTW">iLTW</a> substituting for him until he returned in March 2019.
</p><p>In April 2019, the team played against and lost to the OpenAI Five, a group of artificial intelligence bots that learned to play the game entirely through machine learning, in a live exhibition series in San Francisco. Later that month, Titouan <a href="/dota2/Sockshka" title="Sockshka">Sockshka</a> Merloz replaced Cristian <a href="/dota2/Ppasarel" title="Ppasarel">ppasarel</a> Banaseanu as team coach. The team earned a direct invite to The International 2019 by finishing in the top 12 of that season's Dota Pro Circuit. There, they went 14-2 in the group stage, advancing through the upper bracket before defeating <a href="/dota2/Team_Liquid" title="Team Liquid">Team Liquid</a> in the grand finals 3-1, making them the first ever repeat champion of an International, being awarded $15.6 million out of the $34 million prize pool.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Trivia">Trivia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/dota2/index.php?title=OG&amp;action=edit&amp;section=18" title="Edit section: Trivia">edit</a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Called "春秋霸主" (Spring and Autumn Overlord) by Chinese fans, for their victories of spring and fall Majors. “Spring and Autumn Period ” means the period of 770 BC to 476 BC in Chinese history.</li>
<li>Called "<a class="extiw" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ophiocordyceps_sinensis" title="wikipedia:Ophiocordyceps sinensis">冬蟲夏草</a>" (Winter and Summer Elimination), for their failures during winter Majors and the summer TI. “Winterworm” and "summerherb" both mean medicinal herbs in Chinese, but their literal meanings sound very weak.</li>
<li>They have won the inaugural Dota 2 Frankfurt Major and Manila Major, becoming the first team to repeat as champions of a Valve-sponsored Dota 2 tournament. OG's winning streak extends to also winning the Boston Major and Kiev Major as well, giving them their third and fourth Dota 2 Major championships in a row.</li>
<li>They are the only organization to have won <a href="/dota2/The_International" title="The International">The International</a> from an <a class="mw-redirect" href="/dota2/The_International/2018/Open_Qualifiers/Europe/1" title="The International/2018/Open Qualifiers/Europe/1">open qualifier</a>.</li>
<li>They are the only organization to have won <a href="/dota2/The_International" title="The International">The International</a> twice, and the first organization to win <a href="/dota2/The_International" title="The International">The International</a> two years in a row.</li>
<li>OG lead Liquipedia's <a href="/dota2/Portal:Statistics#By_organization" title="Portal:Statistics">organization money ranking</a>.</li>
<li>They hold the record for most Valve-sponsored tournaments won (totaling 7).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template" style="margin-left:0.1em; white-space:nowrap;">[<i><a class="extiw" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manual_of_Style/Dates_and_numbers#Chronological_items" title="wikipedia:Manual of Style/Dates and numbers"><span title="Needs to be checked after every Valve-sponsored tournament (November 2019)">needs update</span></a></i>]</sup></li></ul>
